E-COMMERCE LANDING PAGE

Delivering a simple and usable website, which guides the potential users through the shopping experience.

TOOLS

Pen and paper, Figma

MY ROLE

I was responsible for creating the landing page for a ecommerce company.

PROJECT'S GOAL -

Present the idea of the shop.

New and fresh design (different from two existing websites).

This website will be a base for the new search engine.

BUSINESS GOALS -

Encourage clients to buy products on the future website.

Increase the knowledge about the Gift Maker's brand.

OVERVIEW -

 







he deep research about the company points out that the company has a wide range of users, starting from young adults to elders. What is more, they sell also based on b2b contracts.









Based on challanges and goals detected during the  research process, I prepare the proposal of a new website design. The landing page is designed using mobile-first approach.

DRIVE PLAN -

As I know, that sometimes it is hard to follow one major methodology and suit all the cases. This time, I had to create my own drive plan.

STAGE 1:

I always start by discovering the problem space. Interviewing people from the team, that I can contact with. Then, looking at competitors. Mapping the case. Gathering everyone's knowledge, thoughts, and needs. Defining the objectives of the prototype.

*Each time, I start to do a task, I need to 'brainstorm' by myself to deeply think about challenges, users, and the whole picture of the app. 

I was asked to design a user experience for an e-commerce website. The idea was to create a one-page website, which will increase the knowleadge what the shop is.

STAGE 2:

In this case, I started with colours and style. From the client point of view, it was a very important thing, and we agreed that I will show some sneak peeks at the beginning.

The brand has already a logo, visual style and two websites. After analyzing the gathered content from interviews and self-investigation, I suggested two colour palettes. 

First one is a raw, fresh and new palette (basing on client needs to introduce something new). The second one based more on facebook colours photos and more connected with the style of other websites (however, still different).

STAGE 3:

Sketching stage, where I draw a sketch of website's information architecture.

STAGE 4:

After the client's feedback, I started developing the design basking on the previously presented sketch.

STAGE 5:

I discussed prototypes with the team. After a few sessions we finalised the project with the below idea:

CONCLUSIONS -

UI design in 2019 should be focused on mobile-first approach.

The less the better. Big white spaces, short and clear content is transparent for all users.

There is always good to introduce landing page for a complex project.

COMMENT -

This project is in the developing phase. The go-live shluld be in the end of 2019.
