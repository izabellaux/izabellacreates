CHARITY PROJECT

Developing the non-profit project, to help my friends from the Elektron Student Organisation, which I used to be a member, to promo their new startup project. The project is focused on designing a website, which would be a teaser for an actual startup website (planned for the future).

TOOLS

Pen and paper, Adobe Illustrator

MY ROLE

I was responsible for creating the "Eqarium" web experience. Creating wireframes, ux content, user experience and ui design. Using Google Design Sprint adjusted to our needs.

PROJECT'S GOAL -

Viewers would be able to know the idea of the Eqarium thought (responsive).

Viewers would be able to contact the people responsible for the activities described.

The UX must be as simple and time-saving as possible.

Client preference is a one-page website.

BUSINESS GOALS -

Encourage students and mentors to take part in the future in this project.

Increase the number of interests in the startup.

Viewers would be able to contact the people responsible for the activities described.

OVERVIEW -

 







I was asked for help to design a website for a startup project. "Equarium" had been a new and fresh idea developed by young people and their mentors. Then, there were many untapped opportunities. I needed to know what I could do, that was going to add the most value for viewers and potential customers.









Student, 23 years old Jack, developing his career in Economic industry. On the other hand - the Mentor, 42 years old businessman who wants to share his knowledge with the younger generation.

DRIVE PLAN -

We followed Google Design Sprint, however as it was an additional project, that is why we changed days into stages.

STAGE 1: Discovering the problem space. Interviewing the startup team and their manager, looking at competitors. Mapping the case. Then, doing How Might We "HMW" on the map. I gathered everyone's knowledge and define the objectives of the prototype.

STAGE 2: Sketch stage, the startup team presented their prototype and I prepared mine. This brought more interesting solutions to the table.

STAGE 3: We decided what we will keep, and what we are going to prototype.?

STAGE 4: I draw out multiple flows and explore the different ideas identified in the design sprint. This was low-fidelity prototypes.

STAGE 5: I met the prototypes with the team members, who tested presented solutions. We agreed on design, features, and information architecture.

RESULTS -

 







As a result, we came up with the diea of RWD website (mobile-first approach). With clear message, nice colour palette (which suits startup colours) and modern design.

CONCLUSIONS -

Everything you see needs a detailed dissections. 

Viewers would be able to contact the people responsible for the activities described.

Over-communicate can lead to miscommunication.

COMMENT -

This project is in the developing phase. The go-live shluld be in the end of 2019.
